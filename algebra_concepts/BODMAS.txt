BODMAS is an acronym used to remember the order of operations in algebra.

- **B**rackets: Simplify expressions inside brackets first.
- **O**rders: Solve exponents (powers and roots, etc.) next.
- **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication: Perform from left to right.
- **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction: Perform last from left to right.

Example:
For the expression 7 + (6 x 5^2) - 4:
1. **Brackets**: 6 x 5^2
2. **Orders**: 6 x 25 = 150
3. **Multiply/Divide**: (No division, so 6 x 25 = 150 stays)
4. **Add/Subtract**: 7 + 150 - 4 = 153
